About Me

I'm an engineering student who started asking questions that biology couldn't answer. This is where that led.

Origin

The Spark

It started with one question:
"Is there scientific proof of the human soul?"

When I couldn't find the answer, the question evolved. How do chemical reactions create thought? How does a biological wet machine produce the experience of being alive?

I studied biomedical engineering and spent years maintaining hospital diagnostic equipment — fixing the machines that kept people alive. But the real question was always underneath: what is the machine doing the maintaining?

Then AI exploded. And I realised something: humanity is currently building a mechanical brain from scratch. By watching how engineers try to replicate intelligence in silicon, I finally had a mirror to understand how we think.

That realisation became The Science Blueprint — a project to document that journey publicly and translate every technical concept I learn into something a curious person can actually hold.

I don't declare. I document. I'm not the professor — I'm the study-buddy who just stayed up too late reading the same paper you did.

How I communicate

The 3 Golden Rules

01
I Share the L's
I document the struggle. If a neuro-paper took me 3 days to understand, I say it. The difficulty is the content — it's what makes it real.
02
I Speak to One Person
I never say "Hey guys." I look at the lens and talk to one intelligent friend who wants to understand, not just to know.
03
I Document, I Don't Declare
I frame everything as "Look at this incredible thing I just figured out" — not "I am the ultimate authority." I'm a student on a journey.
